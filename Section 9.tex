\section{Quasicoherent Sheaves on Algebraic Stacks}\label{sec: quasicoherent sheaves on stacks}
We work over a fixed base scheme $S$ and begin by defining the category $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$ of $\Xcal$ algebraic spaces for $\Xcal$ an $S$-algebraic stack. 
\begin{definition}[$\Spaces_{\Xcal}$]\label{def: Xcal spaces}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The category of $\Xcal$-spaces, $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$ 
    \begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
        \item has objects $(T,t)$ where $T$ is an $S$-algebraic space and $t:T\to\Xcal$ an $S$-morphism
        \item has morphisms $(f,f^{b})$ where $f:T'\to T$ is an $S$-morphism of algebraic spaces and $f^{b}:t'\to t\circ f$ is a natural isomorphism of $S$-morphisms $T'\to\Xcal$ such that for 
        $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCIoVCcnLHQnJykiXSxbMiwwLCIoVCcsdCcpIl0sWzQsMCwiKFQsdCkiXSxbMSwyLCIoZixmXntifSkiXSxbMCwxLCIoZyxnXntifSkiXV0=
        \begin{tikzcd}
            {(T'',t'')} && {(T',t')} && {(T,t)}
            \arrow["{(f,f^{b})}", from=1-3, to=1-5]
            \arrow["{(g,g^{b})}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
        \end{tikzcd}$$
        \begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
            \item $f\circ g:T''\to T$ is an $S$-morphism of algebraic spaces
            \item and 
            $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJ0JyciXSxbMiwwLCJ0J1xcY2lyYyBnIl0sWzQsMCwidFxcY2lyYyBmXFxjaXJjIGciXSxbMCwxLCJnXntifSJdLFsxLDIsImcoZl57Yn0pIl1d
            \begin{tikzcd}
                {t''} && {t'\circ g} && {t\circ f\circ g}
                \arrow["{g^{b}}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
                \arrow["{g(f^{b})}", from=1-3, to=1-5]
            \end{tikzcd}$$
            are natural isomorphisms of $S$-morphisms $T''\to\Xcal$.
        \end{enumerate}
    \end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
    For $(f,f^{b}):(T',t')\to(T,t)$ a morphism in $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$, we think of $f^{b}:t\to t\circ f$ a an isomorphism in $\Xcal(T')$ using the 2-Yoneda lemma. 
\end{remark}
\begin{remark}
    If the algebraic stack $\Xcal$ is in fact an algebraic space, the category $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$ is equivalent to the slice category $\Spaces_{(-/\Xcal)}$. 
\end{remark}
One can naturally restrict the category $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$ to get a category of $\Xcal$-schemes $\Sch_{\Xcal}$. 
\begin{definition}[$\Sch_{\Xcal}$]\label{def: Xcal schemes}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The category of $\Xcal$-schemes, $\Sch_{\Xcal}$ is the full subcategory of $\Spaces_{\Xcal}$ with objects schemes. 
\end{definition}
We can in fact make a similar construction in the category of stacks. 
\begin{definition}[$\Zcal$-Morphism of Stacks]\label{def: relative morphism of stacks}
    Let $\Zcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack and $f:\Xcal\to\Zcal,g:\Ycal\to\Zcal$ be two morphisms of stacks. A $\Zcal$-morphism of stacks $\Xcal\to\Ycal$ is a pair $(h,\sigma)$ with $h:\Xcal\to\Ycal$ a morphism of stacks and a natural isomorphism of stack morphisms $\sigma:f\to g\circ h$.
\end{definition}
This is perhaps more easily seen as the data of the following commutative diagram.

	\arrow["h", from=1-1, to=1-3]
	\arrow["\sigma"{description}, shorten <=8pt, shorten >=8pt, Rightarrow, 2tail reversed, from=0, to=1-3]

Note here that in general stacks are 2-categories so their $\Hom$-sets are in fact 1-categories, giving us a natural notion of the category of morphisms between two stacks over a base stack. 
\begin{definition}[The Category $\Hom_{\Zcal}(\Xcal,\Ycal)$]\label{def: hom category of stacks over a base}
    Let $\Zcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack and $f:\Xcal\to\Zcal,g:\Ycal\to\Zcal$ be two morphisms of stacks. The category of $\Zcal$-morphisms $\Hom_{\Zcal}(\Xcal,\Ycal)$ has objects $(h,\sigma)$ with morphisms between two objects $\lambda:(h,\sigma)\to(h',\sigma')$ a natural isomorphism of $S$-morphisms $\lambda:h\to h'$ such that the diagram of stack morphisms $\Xcal\to\Zcal$
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJmIl0sWzIsMCwiZ35cXGNpcmN+aCJdLFsyLDEsImd+XFxjaXJjfmgnIl0sWzEsMiwiXFxsYW1iZGEiXSxbMCwyLCJcXHNpZ21hJyIsMl0sWzAsMSwiXFxzaWdtYSciXV0=
    \begin{tikzcd}
        f && {g\circ h} \\
        && {g\circ h'}
        \arrow["\lambda", from=1-3, to=2-3]
        \arrow["{\sigma'}"', from=1-1, to=2-3]
        \arrow["{\sigma}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
    \end{tikzcd}$$
    commutes. 
\end{definition}
However, if in in \Cref{def: hom category of stacks over a base}, the morphisms $f:\Xcal\to\Zcal$ and $g:\Ycal\to\Zcal$ are representable, the isomorphism $\lambda$ is determined uniquely giving us the construction of a 1-category of relative spaces over $\Zcal$. More explicitly, if $\lambda$ is unique then for a triplet given by the additional data of $\lambda:(h,\sigma)\to(h',\sigma')$ and $\lambda':(h',\sigma')\to(h'',\sigma'')$ we have

	\arrow["{\sigma''}"'{pos=0.7}, from=1-1, to=3-3]

We then define the category of relative spaces as follows. 
\begin{definition}[$\RelSpaces_{\Zcal}$]\label{def: relative spaces over Zcal}
    Let $\Zcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The category $\RelSpaces_{\Zcal}$ has objects representable morphisms of stacks $(\Xcal\to\Zcal)$ and whose morphisms between $(\Xcal\to\Zcal)$ and $(\Ycal\to\Zcal)$ are $\Zcal$-morphisms of stacks $\Xcal\to\Ycal$. 
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
    One could equivalently define the morphisms in $\RelSpaces_{\Zcal}$ between representable morphisms $(\Xcal\to\Zcal)$ and $(\Ycal\to\Zcal)$ as commuting triangles of the following type:
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJcXFhjYWwiXSxbMiwwLCJcXFljYWwiXSxbMSwxLCJcXFpjYWwiXSxbMCwyXSxbMSwyXSxbMCwxXV0=
    \begin{tikzcd}
        \Xcal && \Ycal \\
        & \Zcal
        \arrow[from=1-1, to=2-2]
        \arrow[from=1-3, to=2-2]
        \arrow[from=1-1, to=1-3]
    \end{tikzcd}$$
\end{remark}
Take caution that while the category of relative spaces $\RelSpaces_{\Zcal}$ has ``spaces'' in the name, it may have objects that are stacks. In particular those stacks $\Xcal$ such that $\Xcal\to\Zcal$ is representable. In particular for $\Spaces_{\Zcal}$ as in \Cref{def: Xcal spaces}, there is an inclusion of 1-categories $\Spaces_{\Zcal}\to\RelSpaces_{\Zcal}$.

$$\left((g,g^{b}):(T',t')\to(T,t)\right)\mapsto\left((g,f\circ g^{b}):(T',f\circ t')\to(T,f\circ t)\right).$$

\subsection{The Lisse-\'{E}tale Site of a Stack}\label{subsec: lisse etale site of a stack}
We can now construct the lisse-\'{e}tale site of an $S$-stack $\Xcal$. 
\begin{definition}[The Lisse-\'{E}tale Site of a Stack]\label{def: lisse etale site of a stack}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The lisse-\'{e}tale site $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ on $\Xcal$ is the full subcategory of $\Sch_{\Xcal}$ with objects $(T,t)$ such that $t:T\to\Xcal$ is a smooth morphism. The Grothendieck topology of $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ is given by coverings 
    $$\left\{(f_{i},f_{i}^{b}):(T_{i},t_{i})\to(T,t)\right\}$$
    where $\{f_{i}:T_{i}\to T\}$ a covering in the small \'{e}tale site on $T$, $T_{\et}$. 
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
    Here $f_{i}^{b}$ and $t_{i}$ defined in the obvious way, with $t_{i}=t\circ f_{i}$ and $f_{i}^{b}:t_{i}\to t\circ f_{i}$ a natural isomorphism such that the diagram 
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJUX3tpfSJdLFsxLDEsIlQiXSxbMiwwLCJcXFhjYWwiXSxbMCwxLCJmX3tpfSIsMl0sWzEsMiwidCIsMl0sWzAsMiwidF97aX0iXSxbNSwxLCJmXntifV97aX0iLDEseyJzaG9ydGVuIjp7InNvdXJjZSI6MTAsInRhcmdldCI6MTB9LCJzdHlsZSI6eyJ0YWlsIjp7Im5hbWUiOiJhcnJvd2hlYWQifX19XV0=
    \begin{tikzcd}
        {T_{i}} && \Xcal \\
        & T
        \arrow["{f_{i}}"', from=1-1, to=2-2]
        \arrow["t"', from=2-2, to=1-3]
        \arrow[""{name=0, anchor=center, inner sep=0}, "{t_{i}}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
        \arrow["{f^{b}_{i}}"{description}, shorten <=2pt, shorten >=2pt, Rightarrow, 2tail reversed, from=0, to=2-2]
    \end{tikzcd}$$
    commutes. 
\end{remark}
A Grothendieck topology allows us to define presheaves and sheaves on a site so we can define the lisse-\'{e}tale topos as follows. 
\begin{definition}[The Lisse-\'{E}tale Topos of a Stack]\label{def: lisse etale topos}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-stack. The lisse-\'{e}tale topos on $\Xcal$, $\Xcal_{\LisEt}$ is the category of sheaves on the lisse-\'{e}tale site $\LisEt(\Xcal)$. 
\end{definition}

$$(h:T'\to T)\mapsto (T',t\circ h)$$
as $t\circ h:T'\to\Xcal$ is a smooth morphism by $h$ \'{e}tale and on morphisms $q:T''\to T'$ over $(h':T''\to T)\to (h':T'\to T)$ by 
$$\left(q:T''\to T'\right)\mapsto\left((\iota_{T}(q),\iota_{T}(q)^{b}):(T'',t\circ h\circ q)\to(T',t\circ h)\right)$$

    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack, $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ the lisse-\'{e}tale site on $\Xcal$, $$\iota_{T}:T_{\et}\to\LisEt(\Xcal)$$ 
    the inclusion functor of the etale site $T_{\et}$ for $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$, and $F:\LisEt(\Xcal)\to\Sets$ a set-valued functor on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$. $F$ is a sheaf of sets on the lisse-\'{e}tale site $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ if and only if $F|_{\iota_{T}(T_{\et})}$ is a sheaf of sets for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$.
\end{lemma}
\subsection{Sheaves on Stacks}\label{subsec: sheaves on stacks}
In algebraic geometry, we saw how each scheme $X$ has a special sheaf $\Ocal_{X}$, its structure sheaf, which we then use to define more complex structures such as quasicoherent sheaves. Developing stack theory in a similar way, we define the structure sheaf of an $S$-algebraic stack $\Xcal$ as follows. 
\begin{definition}[Structure Sheaf of a Stack]\label{def: structure sheaf of stack}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The structure sheaf $\Ocal_{\Xcal}$ is a functor 
    $$\Ocal_{\Xcal}:\LisEt(\Xcal)^{\Opp}\to\Rings$$
    such that $(T,t)\mapsto\Gamma(T,\Ocal_{T})$. 
\end{definition}
This is evidently compatible with morphisms: for $(f,f^{b}):(T',t')\to(T,t)$ the diagram 
$$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsNCxbMCwwLCIoVCcsdCcpIl0sWzIsMCwiKFQsdCkiXSxbMCwxLCJcXEdhbW1hKFQnLCBcXE9jYWxfe1QnfSkiXSxbMiwxLCJcXEdhbW1hKFQsXFxPY2FsX3tUfSkiXSxbMCwxLCIoZixmXntifSkiXSxbMSwzXSxbMCwyXSxbMywyXV0=
\begin{tikzcd}
	{(T',t')} && {(T,t)} \\
	{\Gamma(T', \Ocal_{T'})} && {\Gamma(T,\Ocal_{T})}
	\arrow["{(f,f^{b})}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
	\arrow[from=1-3, to=2-3]
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=2-1]
	\arrow[from=2-3, to=2-1]
\end{tikzcd}$$
commutes taking $\Gamma(T,\Ocal_{T})$ to $\Gamma(f(T),\Ocal_{T'})\subseteq\Gamma(T',\Ocal_{T'})$.
Herein let $\Fcal\in\Xcal_{\LisEt}$, $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$, and $\Fcal_{(T,t)}$ the restriction of the sheaf $\Fcal$ to $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$, that is, $\Fcal\left((T,t)\right)\in\mathrm{Obj}(\Rings)$ as a functor. 
\begin{definition}[Cartesian Sheaf of $\Lambda$-Modules]\label{def: cartesian sheaf of Lambda modules}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack, $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ the lisse-\'{e}tale site on $\Xcal$, 
    $$\Lambda:\LisEt(\Xcal)^{\Opp}\to\Rings$$
    a sheaf of rings on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$, and $\Fcal$ of $\Lambda$-modules on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$. The sheaf of $\Lambda$-modules $\Fcal$ is Cartesian if for all $(f,f^{b}):(T',t')\to(T,t)$ the morphism of $\Lambda_{(T',t')}$-modules
    $$f^{*}\Fcal_{(T,t)}:f^{-1}\Fcal_{(T,t)}\otimes_{(f^{-1}\Lambda_{(T,t)})}\Lambda_{(T',t')}\longrightarrow\Fcal_{(T',t')}$$
    is an isomorphism. 
\end{definition}
A quasicoherent sheaf on a stack is defined to be a Cartesian sheaf in the sense of \Cref{def: cartesian sheaf of Lambda modules} such that it is a quasicoherent sheaf of $\Ocal_{T}$-modules for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$. 
\begin{definition}[Quasicoherent Sheaf on a Stack]
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack, $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ the lisse-\'{e}tale site on $\Xcal$, 
    $$\Lambda:\LisEt(\Xcal)^{\Opp}\to\Rings$$
    a sheaf of rings on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$, and $\Fcal$ of $\Lambda$-modules on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$. The sheaf of $\Lambda$-modules $\Fcal$ is a quasicoherent sheaf if $\Fcal$ is a Cartesian sheaf of $\Lambda$-modules and $\Fcal_{(T,t)}$ is a quasicoherent sheaf of $\Ocal_{T}$-modules for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$.
\end{definition} 
As in the case of schemes, coherent sheaves require an additional Noetherian hypothesis. 
\begin{definition}[Locally Noetherian Stack]\label{def: locally Noetherian stack}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack, $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ the lisse-\'{e}tale site on $\Xcal$. $\Xcal$ is a locally Noetherian algebraic stack if the scheme $T$ is locally Noetherian for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$.
\end{definition}
We can now define coherent sheaves on algebraic stacks. 
\begin{definition}[Coherent Sheaf on a Stack]
    Let $\Xcal$ be a locally Noetherian $S$-algebraic stack, $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ the lisse-\'{e}tale site on $\Xcal$, 
    $$\Lambda:\LisEt(\Xcal)^{\Opp}\to\Rings$$
    a sheaf of rings on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$, and $\Fcal$ of $\Lambda$-modules on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$. The sheaf of $\Lambda$-modules $\Fcal$ is a coherent sheaf if $\Fcal$ is a quasicoherent sheaf of $\Lambda$-modules on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ and $\Fcal_{(T,t)}$ is a finitely generated $\Lambda_{(T,t)}$-module for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$.
\end{definition}
We can then construct the category of quasicoherent sheaves on the $S$-algebraic stack $\Xcal$ which we denote $\QCoh(\Xcal)$ with objects quasicoherent sheaves on $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ and morphisms $\LisEt(\Xcal)$-preserving 2-functors $\Fcal\to\Gcal$ such that the diagram 
$$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJcXEZjYWwiXSxbMiwwLCJcXEdjYWwiXSxbMSwxLCJcXExpc0V0KFxcWGNhbCkiXSxbMCwxXSxbMSwyXSxbMCwyXV0=
\begin{tikzcd}
	\Fcal && \Gcal \\
	& {\LisEt(\Xcal)}
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=1-3]
	\arrow[from=1-3, to=2-2]
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=2-2]
\end{tikzcd}$$
commutes. There is a natural embedding of topoi into the lisse-\'{e}tale topos as in \Cref{def: lisse etale topos} $\Xcal_{\LisEt}$
$$\QCoh(\Xcal)\hookrightarrow\Xcal_{\LisEt}.$$
\begin{proposition}\label{prop: coherence by section}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack (resp. locally Noetherian $S$-algebraic stack), $\Fcal$ a Cartesian sheaf of $\Ocal_{\Xcal}$-modules, and $x:X\to\Xcal$ a smooth surjective map with $X$ a scheme. $\Fcal$ is a quasicoherent sheaf of $\Ocal_{\Xcal}$-modules (resp. coherent sheaf of $\Ocal_{\Xcal}$-modules) if and only if $\Fcal_{(X,x)}$ is a quasicoherent sheaf of $\Ocal_{X}$-modules (resp. is a coherent sheaf of of $\Ocal_{X}$-modules).
\end{proposition}
\begin{remark}
    The use of notation $\Ocal_{X}$-modules is justified as we have isomorphisms 
    $$(\Ocal_{\Xcal})_{(X,x)}=\Gamma(X,\Ocal_{X})=\Ocal_{X}(X).$$
\end{remark}
\begin{proof}
    Let $(Z,z)\in\Obj\left(\LisEt(\Xcal)\right)$ be another object and consider the following Cartesian square. 
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsNCxbMCwwLCJYXFx0aW1lc197XFxYY2FsfVoiXSxbMCwxLCJaIl0sWzIsMCwiWCJdLFsyLDEsIlxcWGNhbCJdLFsxLDMsInoiLDJdLFsyLDMsIngiXSxbMCwxLCJwIiwyXSxbMCwyLCJxIl1d
    \begin{tikzcd}
        {X\times_{\Xcal}Z} && X \\
        Z && \Xcal
        \arrow["z"', from=2-1, to=2-3]
        \arrow["x", from=1-3, to=2-3]
        \arrow["p"', from=1-1, to=2-1]
        \arrow["q", from=1-1, to=1-3]
    \end{tikzcd}$$
    Note that $p$ is smooth as it is obtained from $x:X\to\Xcal$ smooth by base change along $z:Z\to\Xcal$. Arguing by restricting to quasicoherent sheaves on schemes \cite[\href{https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/05JU}{Tag 05JU}]{stacks-project} there is an \'{e}tale surjection $\pi:Z'\to Z$ and $s:Z'\to X\times_{\Xcal}Z$ such that the diagram 
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsNSxbMiwwLCJYXFx0aW1lc197XFxYY2FsfVoiXSxbMiwxLCJaIl0sWzQsMCwiWCJdLFs0LDEsIlxcWGNhbCJdLFswLDAsIlonIl0sWzEsMywieiIsMl0sWzIsMywieCJdLFswLDIsInEiXSxbMCwxLCJwIiwyXSxbNCwwLCJzIl0sWzQsMSwiXFxwaSIsMl1d
    \begin{tikzcd}
        {Z'} && {X\times_{\Xcal}Z} && X \\
        && Z && \Xcal
        \arrow["z"', from=2-3, to=2-5]
        \arrow["x", from=1-5, to=2-5]
        \arrow["q", from=1-3, to=1-5]
        \arrow["p"', from=1-3, to=2-3]
        \arrow["s", from=1-1, to=1-3]
        \arrow["\pi"', from=1-1, to=2-3]
    \end{tikzcd}$$
    commutes. Thus we have that $\Fcal_{(Z,z)}$ is quasicoherent (resp. coherent) if and only if $\pi^{*}\Fcal_{(Z,z)}=\Fcal_{(Z',z\circ\pi)}$  is quasicoherent (resp. coherent). It thus suffices to verify that $\Fcal_{(Z,z)}$ is quasicoherent when $z$ factors in the following way 
    $$% https://q.uiver.app/#q=WzAsMyxbMCwwLCJaIl0sWzIsMCwiWCJdLFs0LDAsIlxcWGNhbCJdLFswLDEsImciXSxbMSwyLCJ4Il0sWzAsMiwieiIsMix7ImN1cnZlIjoyfV1d

        \arrow["g", from=1-1, to=1-3]

    \end{tikzcd}$$
    in which case we have $\Fcal_{(Z,z)}\cong g^{*}\Fcal_{(X,x)}$ since $\Fcal$ is Cartesian giving the claim. 
\end{proof}
\subsection{The \'{E}tale Site of a Stack}\label{subsec: etale site of a stack}
We defined the lisse-\'{e}tale site on an $S$-algebraic stack $\Xcal$ in \Cref{def: lisse etale site of a stack} as the category of schemes admitting a smooth morphism to $\Xcal$ with the data of coverings roughly those of the small \'{e}tale site on each scheme. We can further restrict this site to those schemes with an \'{e}tale morphism to the stack $\Xcal$ to define the \'{e}tale site on the stack. 
\newpage
\begin{definition}[\'{E}tale Site on a Stack]\label{def: etale site of a stack}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. The \'{e}tale site $\Et(\Xcal)$ on $\Xcal$ is the full subcategory of $\Sch_{\Xcal}$ with objects $(T,t)$ such that $t:T\to\Xcal$ is an \'{e}tale morphism. The Grothendieck topology of $\Et(\Xcal)$ is given by coverings 
    $$\left\{(f_{i},f_{i}^{b}):(T_{i},t_{i})\to(T,t)\right\}$$
    where $\{f_{i}:T_{i}\to T\}$ is a covering in the small \'{e}tale site on $T$, $T_{\et}$. 
\end{definition}
As in the case of the lisse-\'{e}tale site, the \'{e}tale topos $\Xcal_{\Et}$ is the category of sheaves on the site $\Et(\Xcal)$ and $\Ocal_{\Xcal_{\Et}}$ the subfunctor of the structure sheaf on the lisse-\'{e}tale site restricted to the full subcategory $\Et(\Xcal)$ of $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ as in \Cref{def: structure sheaf of stack}. Naturally, a sheaf of $\Ocal_{\Xcal_{\Et}}$-modules $\Fcal$ is quasicoherent (resp. coherent) if $\Fcal_{(T,t)}$ is a sheaf of quasicoherent $\Ocal_{T}$-modules (resp. sheaf of coherent $\Ocal_{T}$-modules) for all $(T,t)\in\Obj\left(\Et(\Xcal)\right)$. 
\\\\
Having defined the \'{e}tale site $\Et(\Xcal)$ as a site whose underlying category is a full subcategory of $\LisEt(\Xcal)$ with the same coverings, there is a natural inclusion of categories and thus sites 
$$\Et(\Xcal)\hookrightarrow\LisEt(\Xcal).$$
However, as previously described, $\Ocal_{\Xcal_{\Et}}$ is a subfunctor and thus subsheaf of $\Ocal_{\Xcal}$ inducing a morphism of ringed topoi 
$$r:\left(\Xcal_{\LisEt},\Ocal_{\Xcal}\right)\to\left(\Xcal_{\Et},\Ocal_{\Xcal_{\Et}}\right).$$
More explicitly, let $\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}$ be category of relative spaces over the stack $\Xcal$ as in \Cref{def: relative spaces over Zcal} and $\Et\left(\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}\right)$ the full subcategory of relative spaces $\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}$ with objects objects \'{e}tale representable morphisms of stacks $(\Ycal\to\Xcal)$ -- here taking algebraic spaces and schemes to be a stack in the tautological way. Similarly define $\LisEt\left(\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}\right)$ following \Cref{def: lisse etale site of a stack} as the full subcategory of relative spaces $\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}$ with objects smooth representable morphisms $(\Ycal\to\Xcal)$ for which there is surjective \'{e}tale morphism $y:Y\to\Ycal$ for $Y$ a scheme and coverings given by jointly surjective \'{e}tale morphisms. There is an inclusion 
$$\Et\left(\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}\right)\hookrightarrow\LisEt\left(\RelSpaces_{\Xcal}\right)$$
which induces a continuous morphism of sites that lifts to a morphism of topoi under certain limit and colimit preserving hypotheses satisfied by this functor (vis. eg. \cite[\href{https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/039Z}{Tag 039Z}]{stacks-project}).
\\\\
On Deligne-Mumford stacks, however, quasicoherent sheaves on the lisse-\'{e}tale site and the \'{e}tale site coincide. 
\begin{proposition}\label{prop: if DM then lisse etale qcoh sheaf is etale qcoh sheaf}
    Let $\Xcal$ be an $S$-algebraic stack. If $\Xcal$ is a Deligne-Mumford stack then the restriction functor on quasicoherent sheaves 
    $$r_{*}:\QCoh\left(\Xcal\right)_{\LisEt}\longrightarrow\QCoh\left(\Xcal\right)_{\Et}$$
    is an equivalence of categories. 
\end{proposition}
